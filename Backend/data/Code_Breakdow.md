## Respondent & Geographic Identifiers
- **NFCsID (1)**: Unique respondent identification number for each survey participant
- **STATEQ (2)**: U.S. state where the respondent resides (coded 1-51 for all states plus D.C.)
- **CENSUSDIV (3)**: Census division classification (1-9 representing New England through Pacific)

## Demographic Characteristics
- **PPAGE (4)**: Respondent's age (in years, continuous)
- **PPGENDER (5)**: Respondent's gender (Male/Female)
- **PPAGEG (6)**: Age group classification by gender (12 categories from Male 18-24 to Female 65+)
- **AA_new_w (8)**: Ethnicity grouping (White non-Hispanic vs. Non-White)
- **A5_2015 (9)**: Highest education level completed (1-7 from "Did not complete high school" to "Post graduate degree")

## Household & Family Information
- **A6 (10)**: Marital status (Married, Single, Separated, Divorced, Widowed)
- **A7 (11)**: Current living arrangements (only adult, with spouse/partner, with parents, etc.)
- **A7A (12)**: Simplified marital status (Married, Living with partner, Single)
- **A8_2021 (14)**: Household annual income (1-10 from "Less than $15,000" to "$300,000 or more")
- **A9 (17)**: Current employment status (Self-employed, full-time, part-time, retired, etc.)
- **A40 (18)**: Additional work for pay in past 12 months (Yes/No)
- **A10 (19)**: Spouse/partner's employment status
- **A10A (20)**: Household retirement status
- **A41 (23)**: Education level of parent(s)/guardian(s) who raised respondent
- **A61 (24)**: Whether respondent has financially dependent parents/older relatives

## Financial Behavior & Attitudes
- **J1 (25)**: Satisfaction with personal financial condition (1-10 scale)
- **J2 (26)**: Willingness to take financial risks (1-10 scale)
- **J3 (27)**: Household spending relative to income (less than, more than, or equal to)
- **J4 (28)**: Difficulty covering expenses and paying bills
- **J40 (29)**: Income stability/variability month-to-month
- **J5 (30)**: Whether respondent has 3-month emergency/rainy day funds
- **J6 (31)**: Whether saving for children's college education
- **J8 (32)**: Whether tried to calculate retirement savings needs
- **J9 (33)**: Pre-retirement calculation of savings needs (for retired respondents)
- **J10 (34)**: Unexpected large drop in income in past 12 months
- **J20 (35)**: Confidence in ability to come up with $2,000 for an emergency
- **J60_1 through J60_99 (36-45)**: Sources for emergency funds (savings, credit cards, loans, etc.)
- **J41_1-3 (52-54)**: Financial stress/concern statements (describes me... scale)
- **J42_1-2 (55-56)**: Money leftover at month-end frequency
- **J64 (57)**: Confidence in achieving financial goals
- **J65 (58)**: Whether targeted in financial fraud/scam in past year
- **J66 (59)**: Whether money was lost to fraud/scam

## Banking & Financial Services
- **B1 (60)**: Whether household has a checking account
- **B2 (61)**: Whether household has savings account, money market, or CDs
- **B4 (62)**: Whether checking account is occasionally overdrawn
- **B41_1 (63)**: Frequency of online banking (computer/laptop)
- **B41_2 (64)**: Frequency of mobile banking (phone)
- **B60 (65)**: Trust in banks to treat fairly (1-7 scale)
- **B31 (66)**: Frequency of mobile payments in-person
- **B42 (67)**: Frequency of mobile money transfers to others
- **B43 (68)**: Frequency of using financial apps for budgeting/credit management

## Retirement & Investments
- **C1_2012 (70)**: Whether has employer-sponsored retirement plans
- **C2_2012 (71)**: Source of retirement plans (own employer, spouse's, both)
- **C3_2012 (72)**: Whether plans allow choice in investments
- **C4_2012 (73)**: Whether has non-employer retirement accounts (IRA, Keogh, etc.)
- **C5_2012 (74)**: Whether regularly contributes to retirement accounts

## Housing & Mortgages
- **E5 (76)**: Home ownership status (own/buying, rent, etc.)
- **E6 (77)**: Type of home (house, condo, mobile home, etc.)
- **E7 (80)**: Whether currently has mortgages on home
- **E8 (81)**: Whether has home equity loans
- **E9 (82)**: Whether owes more on home than it could be sold for (underwater)
- **E15_2015 (83)**: Frequency of late mortgage payments (past 12 months)
- **E60 (84)**: Whether increased housing costs caused spending cuts

## Credit Cards & Debt
- **F1 (88)**: Number of credit cards owned
- **F2_1-6 (89-94)**: Credit card behaviors (paid in full, carried balance, minimum payment only, late fees, over-limit fees, cash advances)
- **G1 (95)**: Whether has auto loan
- **G20 (96)**: Whether has past-due medical bills
- **G30_1-99 (97-104)**: Student loan status by borrower (self, spouse, child, etc.)
- **G35 (105)**: Frequency of late student loan payments
- **G38 (113)**: Whether contacted by debt collection agency (past 12 months)
- **G23 (114)**: Agreement with "I have too much debt right now" (1-7 scale)

## Alternative Financial Services
- **G25_1-5 (107-111)**: Use of alternative financial services (auto title loans, payday loans, tax refund advances, pawn shops, rent-to-own)
- **G60 (112)**: Whether used "Buy Now Pay Later" in last 12 months

## Health & Insurance
- **H1 (115)**: Whether covered by health insurance
- **H30_3 (116)**: Whether avoided medical care due to cost (past 12 months)

## Financial Knowledge & Education
- **M1_1 (117)**: Self-assessed ability with day-to-day financial matters (1-7 scale)
- **M4 (118)**: Self-assessed overall financial knowledge (1-7 scale)
- **M20 (119)**: Whether financial education was offered and/or taken
- **M21_1-4 (120-123)**: Timing of financial education (high school, college, workplace, other)
- **M22 (124)**: Whether financial education was mandatory or voluntary
- **M6 (125)**: Big Three financial literacy question 1 (interest/compound interest)
- **M7 (126)**: Big Three financial literacy question 2 (inflation/purchasing power)
- **M8 (127)**: Big Three financial literacy question 3 (risk diversification)
- **M11 (128)**: Numeracy question (probability/risk interpretation)
- **M9 (129)**: Mortgage knowledge (15-year vs. 30-year comparison)
- **M10 (130)**: Investment knowledge (single stock vs. mutual fund risk)

## Weights
- **wgt_n2 (131)**: National-level weight for representative U.S. population estimates
- **wgt_d2 (132)**: Census Division-level weight for division-level estimates
- **wgt_s3 (133)**: State-level weight for state-level estimates
